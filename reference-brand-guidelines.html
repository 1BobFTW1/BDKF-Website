Brand Guidelines

Version 1.0 · The corner-letter system
01

The system in one sentence

Everything in this identity is generated by a single geometric rule. If you remember nothing else from this document, remember this:

The letters B, D, K, F sit on the four corners of one rectangle. On small formats the rectangle collapses into the Mark. On large formats it opens to the page margins and becomes the Frame. All four letters are always present and always fully visible. No exceptions.

Quadrant identity

B is only ever top-left. D top-right. K bottom-left. F bottom-right. This holds in the Mark and in the Frame, so both states spell BDKF in natural reading order (Z-pattern). Letters never trade places.

Two states, one object

The Mark and the Frame are not two logos — they are the same rectangle at two sizes. Never design a third lockup (no horizontal "BDKF" wordmark, no stacked variant). If a space can't hold the Frame, use the Mark.

Full visibility

A letter may never be cropped, covered, replaced by an image, or absorbed into another element. If a layout hides even one letter, the identity is broken — a passer-by must always be able to read all four.

02

The Mark (collapsed state)

The Mark is the 2×2 letter block. It is the logo for small applications: business cards, favicons, app icons, stamps, social avatars, and as a compact sign-off on dense layouts (e.g. brochure interiors).

03

The Frame (opened state)

The Frame is the four letters at the corners of the format. All measurements derive from one base unit so the system scales to any size and proportion without judgement calls: U = 1% of the format's shorter edge.

Portrait example — the same formulas apply to square, landscape, and 9:16
PropertySpecification

MarginM = 6U — identical on all four sides, measured, never eyeballed
Letter sizeL = 10U cap height. All four letters identical size & weight

Exclusion zoneEach letter's bounding box + 0.5L padding on all sides. Nothing enters it — not text, not the cat, not photography
Content areaEverything inside the margins minus the four exclusion zones
Minimum formatShorter edge ≥ 100 mm print / 600 px screen. Below that, use the Mark instead

Why the exclusion zone exists

Earlier drafts let the cat and headlines drift into the corners, which crowded or covered letters. The 0.5L zone makes the rule mechanical: if anything touches the amber area, move or shrink the content — never the letters.

04

Scale discipline

Max three type sizes per piece: one headline, one body, one label. More sizes = visual noise.

Case logic

Headlines and labels are uppercase; body copy is sentence case. Never letterspace the headline — only labels get tracking.

Fallback

Where Forma DJR Deck can't load (e.g. plain-text email), fall back to Helvetica / Arial. Never substitute a decorative or rounded font to "add fun".

05

Colour

The identity is black-and-white. Amber exists for exactly one job: it is the colour of the cat's eyes, and by extension the single highlight per piece (one date, one CTA, one word). If amber appears twice in a layout, it appears once too often.

Inversion

Any layout may run inverted (Paper letters on Ink ground) — the measurements don't change. The cat's eyes stay amber in both. The four letters are only ever Ink or Paper — never amber, never grey, never in a photo texture.

06

The cat

The story of the geometry: the letters are the Freunde standing at the corners; the cat is the Katze, safe in the space they hold. The cat therefore always lives inside the content area — it peeks in from an edge, but it never touches, covers, or replaces a letter.

Placement

Enters from the bottom or a side edge of the content area, between the exclusion zones. Width on posters/flyers: 36–46U. Only one cat per piece.

Clearance

Minimum 0.5L between the cat's silhouette and any exclusion zone — the same padding rule as everything else. If it's tight, shrink the cat.

Colour

Solid Ink silhouette, amber eyes with Ink pupils. On Ink backgrounds: Paper silhouette, eyes stay amber. The cat is never outlined, patterned, or photographic.

09

Pre-flight checklist

Before anything ships, every piece answers yes to all seven:

1Are all four letters present, uncovered, and fully inside the format?
2Is B top-left, D top-right, K bottom-left, F bottom-right?
3Do all four letters share one size (L) and one weight (Extra Bold)?
4Is the margin M identical on all four sides — measured, not eyeballed?
5Is every exclusion zone empty — no text, cat, or imagery inside?
6Is everything set in Forma DJR Deck, in at most three sizes?
7Does amber appear at most once outside the cat's eyes?
